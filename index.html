AI-Based Multimodal Estimation of eGFR Using Kidney Ultrasound and Clinical Parameters

You are being invited to take part in a research study titled “AI-Based Multimodal Estimation of eGFR Using Kidney Ultrasound and Clinical Parameters”. Before you decide, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with friends, relatives and your doctors if you wish. Ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Thank you for reading this.

  1. What is the purpose of the study?

    The purpose of this study is to develop and test an Artificial Intelligence (AI) model that can detect and predict chronic kidney diseases at an early stage.

  2. Why have I been chosen?

    You have been chosen to participate because you had a kidney ultrasound and kidney-related laboratory tests as part of your clinical care. The clinical information you provide is good enough to train and test the AI model. The information is provided voluntarily.

  3. Do I have to take part?

    Your participation is completely voluntary and will not affect your level of medical care at the Health Centre. You may opt not to participate in the study at any time.

  4. What will happen to me if I take part?

    If you agree to participate, you will allow the research team to access and use

    (i) Your kidney ultrasound images, and
    (ii) Your laboratory reports related to kidney function

    No extra procedures or examinations will be required.

  5. What do I have to do?

    You will not be required to attend any extra appointments or undergo additional tests. Your anonymized data will be used for research purposes. No identifiable information will be accessed, and you will not be contacted.

  6. What are the possible risks of taking part?

    Participation in this research study does not involve any known risks.

  7. What are the possible benefits of taking part?

    You may not receive direct personal benefit from taking part in this study. But your participation may contribute to the development of improved tools for early detection of chronic kidney diseases. The findings could support earlier diagnosis, better monitoring, and more effective treatment strategies for future patients.

  8. Will my taking part in this study be kept confidential?

    Your information will be handled in a confidential manner and protected according to institutional policies and applicable laws. The research dataset will no longer contain your name or any other direct identifiers. Data will be kept in a safe, password-protected system with controlled access.

  9. What will happen to the results of the research study?

    The findings of the study may be published or discussed in conferences or at scientific meetings. Only a collection of findings will be described in these results. Your private medical information will not be disclosed, and no specific participant will be identified. The results of the research study are used to improve early detection of chronic kidney disease.

    About 20% of the total data, collected in an irreversible anonymised manner, will be part of benchmarking and open data initiative of TANUH for evaluation of AI algorithms.

  10. Who is organising and funding the research?

    The research study is being organized at the TANUH, AI Centre of Excellence, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore 560012 and funded by a research grant from Ministry of Education, Government of India.

  11. Who has reviewed the study?

    The research study has been reviewed by the CLINICOM, Independent Ethics Committee for Evaluation of Protocols for Clinical Research, Bangalore 560003.

  12. Who can I contact for further information?

    If you have any questions about the study, you may contact Dr. S. Hema Priyadarshini, hema.priya@tanuh.ai, +91 9945334356.

    If you wish to speak to someone independent of the study as well as withdrawal of the consent, you may contact the Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC)

Recalibration and Validation of the Kidney Failure Risk Equation in an Indian Chronic Kidney Disease Cohort

You are being invited to take part in a research study titled “Recalibration and Validation of the Kidney Failure Risk Equation in an Indian Chronic Kidney Disease Cohort”. Before you decide, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with friends, relatives and your doctors if you wish. Ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Thank you for reading this.

  1. What is the purpose of the study?

    The purpose of this study is to improve the prediction of kidney failure risk in Indian patients with chronic kidney disease by adapting an existing risk prediction model using Indian patient data.

  2. Why have I been chosen?

    You have been chosen to participate because your medical records include kidney-related tests that are useful for understanding kidney health. The clinical information in the records is sufficient to train and test the AI model to assess the kidney function.

  3. Do I have to take part?

    Your participation is completely voluntary and will not affect your level of medical care at the Health Centre. You may opt not to participate in the study at any time.

  4. What will happen to me if I take part?

    If you agree to participate, you will allow the research team to access and use your laboratory reports related to kidney function. These data will be de-identified and analyzed using artificial intelligence-based methods to develop and validate models for estimating kidney function by estimating an individual’s 2-year and 5-year risk of progression to kidney failure.

  5. What do I have to do?

    You will not be required to attend any extra appointments or undergo additional tests. Your anonymized data will be used for research purposes. No identifiable information will be accessed, and you will not be contacted.

  6. What are the possible risks of taking part?

    Participation in this research study does not involve any known risks.

  7. What are the possible benefits of taking part?

    You may not receive direct personal benefit from participating in this study, as no changes will be made to your medical care based on the study findings. However, the knowledge gained may help improve assessment and management of kidney disease for future patients.

  8. Will my taking part in this study be kept confidential?

    Your information will be handled in a confidential manner and protected according to institutional policies and applicable laws. The research dataset will no longer contain your name or any other direct identifiers. Data will be kept in a safe, password-protected system with controlled access.

  9. What will happen to the results of the research study?

    The study results will be used for research and academic purposes to improve prediction of kidney failure risk in Indian CKD patients. Results may be published or presented in summary form, without revealing any personal identity. About 20% of the total data, collected in an irreversible anonymised manner, will be part of benchmarking and open data initiative of TANUH for evaluation of AI algorithms.

  10. Who is organising and funding the research?

    The research study is being organized at the TANUH, AI Centre of Excellence, Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore 560012 and funded by a research grant from Ministry of Education, Government of India.

  11. Who has reviewed the study?

    The research study has been reviewed by the CLINICOM, Independent Ethics Committee for Evaluation of Protocols for Clinical Research, Bangalore 560003.

  12. Who can I contact for further information?

    If you have any questions about the study you may contact Dr. S. Hema Priyadarshini, hema.priya@tanuh.ai, +91 9945334356.

    If you wish to speak to someone independent of the study as well as withdrawal of the consent, you may contact the Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC).
